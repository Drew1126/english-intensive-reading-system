Ninety minutes. Ninety days. One goal.

We all have that one big goal — the book we've been meaning to write, the side hustle we want to launch, the course we keep saying we'll finish. But somehow, between emails, meetings, and doomscrolling, it keeps getting pushed to "tomorrow." That's where the 90/90/1 rule comes in.

The 90/90/1 rule is a simple productivity strategy designed to cut through the chaos and help you finally make progress. The concept was developed by Robin Sharma in his book "The 5 AM Club." If you want to make progress toward a goal, Sharma recommends spending the first 90 minutes of your workday focused on that 1 goal, for 90 days in a row.

The 90/90/1 rule draws on multiple cognitive and psychological principles, making it an effective productivity strategy. These principles include:

Research shows that sticking with a new behavior — especially as part of your morning routine — can make it easier to turn into a lasting habit.

Working on a project for 90 days straight helps you build the momentum you need to keep going. Otherwise, you might work on it for a day or two, then lose track of it for weeks or months.

The progress you accomplish serves as a powerful motivator to keep at it.

The rule promotes single-tasking by requiring you to give your full attention to just one goal. When you concentrate on one thing, you can complete it much more efficiently than when you're trying to switch between several tasks.

Concentrating deeply for 90 minutes without distractions allows your brain to become fully immersed in the task and enter a flow state of heightened focus and attention.

Research shows that we are 500% more productive in a flow state than otherwise.
